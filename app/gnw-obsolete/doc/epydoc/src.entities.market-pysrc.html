 15      """ 
 16      The L{Market} entity's sole role is to 
 17      balance the sum of all in flows with the 
 18      sum of all out flows. Typically, the L{Market} 
 19      entity is linked to many L{StandardProduct} 
 20      entities covering the entire L{DISPATCH_PERIODS} 
 21      with their delivery periods and to one or more 
 22      other entities that have some optimisation 
 23      potential (such as a Storage, Supplier, Consumer). 
 24      In most cases, all entities the L{Market} entity 
 25      is linked with are linked as source entity as 
 26      well as sink entity (but other constellations 
 27      are possible). 
 28       
 29      In order to construct a L{Market} entity that supports 
 30      volume dependent bid/ask price spreads the L{Market} 
 31      entity links, for each standard product delivery period, 
 32      to a corresponding L{StandardProduct} entity, that itself 
 33      links to multiple L{TradeTranche} entities that have 
 34          - restricted capacity limits, and 
 35          - a widening spread between bid/ask prices. 
 36      This will cause the optimiser to transact volumes for 
 37      the given standard product first at the most favourable 
 38      trade tranche up to its upper capacity limit, before the 
 39      second most favourable trade tranche is considered, etc. 
 40      The least favourable trade tranche shouldn't have an upper 
 41      capacity limit set and have a punishing bid/ask price spread 
 42      and serves as so called 'slack' tranche.   
 43   
 44      @todo: Add features to the L{Market} entity that allows 
 45      check for validity of all linked to source and sink entities, 
 46      such as: 
 47          - test the L{DISPATCH_PERIODS} are fully covered by 
 48          L{StandardProduct} delivery periods 
 49          - test that L{StandardProduct} delivery periods do not 
 50          overlap (in a first instance, later this restriction 
 51          may have to be relaxed) 
 52      """
